01 What we do

Your board said "do something about AI." We come in and make that real.

2weeks Audit
90days Plan
1quarter Shipped

You're a 50–500 person company in the UAE or GCC. Your board, your CEO, or your own gut is telling you AI matters now. Your team is split — half thinks you're already doing it, half has no idea where to start.

We assess where you actually are. We pick the right first use case for your business, not the one in last week's headlines. We stand up the taskforce. We supervise the build. You ship something real in a quarter, not in two years.

We are not generalist consultants. We are not a tools vendor. We are an operator-led AI adoption practice — a small group of principals, each running this playbook inside the company they work for, available to run it inside yours.

02 How we work

Four ways in. Pick the one that matches where you are.

01 · 2 weeks · free

AI Readiness Audit

We score you across data, team, process, tooling, leadership and use-case readiness. You leave with a 12-page exec readout and a prioritized 90-day plan.

  • 12-page exec readout — no jargon, six dimensions scored
  • 6-dimension diagnostic — data, team, process, tooling, leadership, use cases
  • Prioritized 90-day plan — the first three moves, ranked
  • Always free — no upsell trap, we quote after
02 · half or full day

AI Taskforce Workshop

A working session, not a presentation. Your team leaves the room with their own AI charter, a backlog, a governance model, and a 30-day plan they can run on Monday.

  • An AI charter — what AI is for at your company, in your words
  • A prioritized backlog — top 10 use cases, scored and sequenced
  • A governance model — who approves what, what stays human-in-the-loop
  • A 30-day plan — Monday morning, your team starts running it
03 · quarterly retainer

Fractional AI Advisor

Embedded with your leadership team between formal engagements — vendor selection, governance, unblocking, hiring inputs. For teams who have a plan and need senior eyes.

  • Vendor + tool selection — which platform, which model, which contract
  • Governance check-ins — your risk register doesn't outrun your usage
  • Hiring inputs — JDs, interview loops, leveling for AI-adjacent roles
  • Weekly call + async — same operator, every week
04 · 10–12 weeks

Adoption Sprint

One real use case, blank page to production. We orchestrate the build, govern the deployment, train your team. You get a shipped capability, a trained operator, and a playbook.

  • One use case shipped — picked together, scoped tight, in production
  • A trained operator — someone inside your company who can run the next one
  • A reusable playbook — use case 2 is faster than this one
  • Exec sponsor required — we'll tell you no if the air-cover isn't there
Engagement pricing is scoped per company. The audit is always free — talk to us first; we'll quote after the call.

Most AI consulting ends with a deck. This ends with something shipped.

03 Training

Or, train your own people. A program for each room.

The same playbook we run inside companies, rewired into a curriculum. Four programs — for the boards who need vocabulary, the operators who need leverage, the builders who need patterns, and the rest of the team who just need fluency.

01 · 1 day · C-suite + board

Executive AI Briefing

Closed-door session for the people writing the cheque. Strategy, capital allocation, governance, where the real risk lives. They leave knowing what to ask their team — and what answers should worry them.

  • Decision frameworks — how to score a vendor pitch in ten minutes
  • Capital allocation — what AI spend looks like for your size of company
  • Governance + risk — the three boards that actually matter
  • Outcome — a leadership team that can sponsor adoption, not just rubber-stamp it
02 · 5 days over 3 weeks · managers + functional leads

AI Operator Bootcamp

Department heads pick a real use case from their own backlog. By the end of the cohort they've scoped it, prototyped it, and pitched it back to the room. You leave with people who can run pilots, not just request them.

  • Use-case scoping — picking the one that won't waste the quarter
  • Prototype to pitch — every cohort ships a working demo
  • Vendor evaluation — the same playbook we use in the audit
  • Outcome — three to five managers who can lead an internal pilot
03 · 4 weeks · engineering + data

AI Builder Track

For the team that has to ship. RAG, agents, evals, guardrails — built against your stack, your data, your latency budget. Ends with one feature pushed to staging and the rest of the team able to ship the next.

  • Hands-on with your stack — no toy datasets, no "imagine if…"
  • Evals + observability — how you'll know it's working in prod
  • Guardrail patterns — what to ship vs. what to gate
  • Outcome — one shipped feature, plus a team that can ship the next three
04 · 6 weeks · whole company

AI Fluency Program

Floor-level competence across the company. Async curriculum plus three live sessions. Tool use, prompt patterns, when not to use AI, and your own internal AI playbook by the end of week six.

  • Tool fluency — what to use for what, with examples from your own work
  • Prompt patterns — five reusable templates, not fifty tricks
  • When not to use AI — the costliest mistake we see every month
  • Outcome — an internal AI playbook the team actually opens
Cohorts run on-site in Dubai or remote across the GCC. Programs scope per company — talk to us about a custom blend across the four.
